Read

Organizing Data

  • Peruse the website “Data Organization in Spreadsheets”, much of which has been written up in a journal article:
    Broman KW, Woo KH (2018) Data organization in spreadsheets. The American Statistician 78:2–10. PDF ( 3.9 MB)


Importing Data

  • R4DS, Ch. 10: Tibbles (short)
  • R4DS, Ch. 11.2: Data Import, “Getting started”
    • I suggest working through the exercises to help you understand the code.

Supplemental Reading

Do you need to read messy files? I suggeset reading the remainder of R4DS, Ch. 11. In particular:

  • Section 11.3, “Parsing a Vector” decribes a variety of functions provided by the readr package to analyze and interpret different kinds of input and figure out what kind of value it’s supposed to be. For example, understanding that the value represents a number when it’s formatted as $123,456,789, or that the value represents a date when it’s formatted as oct. 14, 1979 or 1 enero 2015.
  • Section 11.4, “Parsing a file” describes strategies for guessing column types during import, diagnosing problems, and overriding the defaults when the guesses fail.
